Specifications & Regulations

Welcome to the Specifications and Regulations section. Here, we compile and display tables detailing tech standardization projects for virtual environments and regulations (laws) that play a crucial role in shaping the metaverse landscape. As developers, understanding these standards and regulations is essential for producing high-quality software and ensuring compliance with legal requirements. Our curated tables provide valuable insights into the latest tech standardization projects, such as specifications for 3D graphics interchange and access to virtual reality devices, as well as relevant regulations aimed at protecting users in the metaverse. By staying informed about these developments, developers can create better software, foster user trust, and contribute to the growth of a safe and vibrant metaverse ecosystem.

Specifications

Project Details
Universal Scene Description A specification for 3D computer graphics interchange created by Pixar and supported by Blender, Apple's SceneKit and Autodesk 3ds Max. The technology company NVIDIA announced in 2021 they would adopt USD for their metaverse development tools.
glTF A specification for the efficient transmission and loading of 3D scenes and models by engines and applications created by the Khronos Group, an industry consortium developing royalty free open standards. In August 2022 it was announced that glTF 2.0 had been released as the ISO/IEC 12113:2022 International Standard.
OpenXR An open standard for access to virtual and augmented reality devices and experiences. It has been adopted by Microsoft for HoloLens 2, Meta Platforms for the Oculus Quest, HTC for the HTC Vive, Qualcomm for the Snapdragon Spaces XR Developer Platform, and Valve for SteamVR.

Regulations

Act Details
Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) The DMCA addresses issues related to copyright infringement on the internet, including provisions for online service providers to address and respond to claims of copyright infringement. Given the potential for user-generated content and intellectual property issues in the metaverse, the DMCA could be relevant.
Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) COPPA regulates the online collection of personal information from children under the age of 13. In virtual environments and social platforms within the metaverse, compliance with COPPA is crucial when dealing with users who are minors.
Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) The CFAA prohibits various forms of computer-related activities, including unauthorized access to computer systems and networks. This act could be relevant to issues such as hacking, cybersecurity, and unauthorized access within the metaverse.
Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA) The ECPA sets standards for government surveillance of electronic communications and provides privacy protections for electronic communications such as email and online messaging. As communication and interaction within the metaverse increasingly occur through digital channels, the ECPA may have implications for privacy rights.
Telecommunications Act of 1996 Implications for communication services, competition, and regulation within the digital sphere, which could impact the metaverse.
Communications Decency Act (CDA) Implications for online communication, content regulation, and liability for internet intermediaries. While certain provisions of the CDA have been subject to legal challenges and amendments over the years, it remains an important law with potential relevance to the regulation of the metaverse.
